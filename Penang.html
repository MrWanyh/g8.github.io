George Town Street Art

About Georgetown Street Art

George Town's street art scene has transformed the UNESCO World Heritage city into an open-air gallery. The artworks, including murals and wrought iron caricatures, beautifully blend with the historic architecture, telling stories of Penang's rich cultural heritage.

The most famous pieces were created during the 2012 George Town Festival, including Ernest Zacharevic's "Little Children on a Bicycle" and "Boy on a Bike". These interactive artworks have become iconic photo spots for visitors.

Visitor Information

  • Location: Throughout George Town's heritage zone
  • Best Time to Visit: Early morning or late afternoon
  • Walking Tour: Self-guided maps available at tourist centers
  • Notable Artists: Ernest Zacharevic, Louis Gan, Julia Volchkova

Tips for Visitors

Wear comfortable walking shoes, bring a camera, and explore beyond the famous murals to discover hidden artworks in back alleys. Respect the artworks and local residents while taking photos.
