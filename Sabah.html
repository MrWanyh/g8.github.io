Mount Kinabalu

About Mount Kinabalu

Mount Kinabalu, at 4,095 meters, is Southeast Asia's highest peak and a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Sabah, Malaysian Borneo. The mountain is sacred to the local Kadazan-Dusun people and is renowned for its remarkable biodiversity, with over 5,000 plant species, 326 birds, and 100 mammal species.

The climb to the summit is challenging but achievable for moderately fit individuals, typically taking 2 days with an overnight stay at Laban Rata. The sunrise view from Low's Peak is one of Malaysia's most breathtaking natural spectacles.

Visitor Information

  • Location: Kinabalu Park, Sabah
  • Climbing Season: Year-round (best March-April)
  • Permit Required: Yes, limited daily
  • Climb Duration: 2 days/1 night standard

Tips for Visitors

Book your climb 3-6 months in advance, pack warm clothing for the summit, and consider altitude acclimatization. Even if not climbing, the Kinabalu Park headquarters offers beautiful nature trails with stunning mountain views.
